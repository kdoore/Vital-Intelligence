# Living Systems in Motion: Two Simulations

This page documents two interactive simulations built to make the VIM framework's dynamic claims visible — not as diagrams, but as living systems you can manipulate in real time. They were built iteratively, each one revealing something the previous version could not show.

***

### How to use this page

Open each simulation. Move the sliders. Watch what happens before reading the explanation. The simulations are designed to produce a felt sense before they produce a named concept — this is intentional, and it is the framework's own pedagogy made operational.

***

### Simulation 1 — VIM Consciousness Field Simulator

**→** [**Launch Simulation 1**](https://claude.ai/public/artifacts/0499102b-6fbf-4e6b-9c9f-e64e15e38e1c)

_Light theme · particle agents · analytical register_

The first simulation renders the VIM instrument panel as a live field. Four sliders — ♠ somatic coherence, ♦ epistemic aperture, ♥ relational trust, ♣ temporal depth — set field conditions that determine which attractor basin the system moves toward. Three agent types are present simultaneously: red extraction agents pulling toward the Giant Pumpkin, green kindness agents seeding the Commitment Pool, and gray neutral agents following the dominant field condition.

**What it shows:**

* The two attractor basins as competing gravitational fields
* The noise bar reading from brown (rigid, S0/S4) to pink (alive, S3) — pink noise is the health signature of complex adaptive systems
* Six MDP state presets that drop the field into each attractor configuration
* Real-time readout of consciousness capital, kindness field strength, and current state

**What to try:** Hit S0 — frozen order. Then slowly raise ♥ relational trust while watching the noise bar. The shift from brown to mixed to pink is the kindness field activating.

### Simulation 2 — Bioluminescent Field

**→** [**Launch Simulation 2**](https://claude.ai/public/artifacts/0267060f-2371-43d6-b8d2-dadedd6d76cc)

_Dark ocean · fluid dynamics · contemplative and demonstrative register_

The second simulation was built in response to what the first one could not show: the _cascade_ — the nonlinear propagation of kindness through a field, one agent recruiting the next. It uses a curl noise flow field rather than particle dynamics, so agents follow invisible ocean currents that shift slowly over time. The movement is governed by fluid dynamics, not random walk.

**What is different:**

**The fish are directional.** Each agent is fish-shaped and faces the direction of travel. Bioluminescent glow scales with consciousness capital — higher-capacity agents glow brighter.

**The cascade is real.** When kindness fish come within range of neutral fish, they gradually shift them — you can watch individual agents convert from grey-teal to green in real time. The kindness cascade meter at the top tracks what fraction of the field has been recruited. Conversion rate scales with ♥ relational trust.

**The MPCM boundary does something.** The faint luminous horizon line is not decorative. Extraction fish feel a soft repulsion from crossing below it; kindness fish receive extra pull toward the Commitment Pool once they cross it. When kindness agents are near the boundary, it visibly brightens.

**Demo mode plays the full arc.** Hit _▶ watch the cascade_ and the simulation runs a scripted sequence: S0 frozen order → T01 first crack → S1 productive disequilibration → T23 nucleation → S3 holarchic flow → cascade deepens. The sliders animate automatically and each phase is labeled. Speed 6–7 gives the clearest teaching arc.

***

### The iterative process

These simulations were built in sequence, each version responding to what the previous one revealed. This is the design methodology the framework itself describes: emergence guided, not predetermined.

**Simulation 1** established the instrument panel as an interactive object and made the attractor basins visible as competing gravitational fields. It showed that the Giant Pumpkin always maintains some density — extraction dynamics are never fully eliminated, only outweighed. This is accurate to how real information environments work.

**What Simulation 1 could not show:** the mechanism of transformation. At S3 with all dials maxed, agents coexisted near both basins but no visible migration occurred. The kindness field was present but the _cascade_ — the nonlinear recruitment dynamic that makes prosocial transformation qualitatively different from mere dial adjustment — was invisible.

**Simulation 2** was built to make the cascade visible. The shift from particle dynamics to fluid dynamics (curl noise flow field) produced the streaming, oceanic quality that makes the field feel like a living system rather than a physics simulation. The fish-shaped agents with directional bodies and trail persistence carry the Haeckel/bioluminescent visual language established in the MDP image series.

***

### An emergent finding: the MPCM boundary and extraction amplification

During testing, an unexpected behavior appeared: **extraction agents (orange-red fish) appear to be amplified after crossing the MPCM boundary, not repelled.**

The intended behavior was repulsion — extraction agents should be discouraged from crossing into the deeper field where the Commitment Pool's influence is strongest. What actually happens: extraction agents with enough momentum from the Giant Pumpkin's gravitational pull cross the boundary anyway, and once below it, the GP's pull continues to operate. The repulsion force is real but it requires a strong kindness field to overcome the attractor momentum.

This is not a bug. It is the framework's own logic made visible:

> **The MPCM boundary only holds when the kindness field is sufficiently strong.**

When ♥ relational trust and ♠ somatic coherence are low, the boundary is permeable to extraction dynamics. When they are high, kindness agents cross freely toward the Commitment Pool while extraction agents are turned back. The boundary's permeability is a function of field conditions — which is precisely what the instruments are designed to cultivate.

**For teaching:** Run the simulation at S0 (low relational trust). Watch whether orange fish cross the boundary. Then raise ♥ relational trust to 80+. Watch whether the boundary behavior changes. The question to hold: _what are the actual field conditions that make a kindness boundary structurally enforceable, rather than merely declared?_

This is a Tier 3 observation — emergent from the simulation, not derived from the formal model. It is offered as a generative probe, not a confirmed claim.

***

### What these simulations cannot show

Both simulations model structural dynamics. Neither can model:

* The felt sense in your body as you watch the field shift
* The specific history of the living system in front of you
* The difference between a kindness field that is performed and one that is structurally present
* The seven-generation temporal horizon — both simulations operate in the immediate present

These are not gaps to fix in the next version. They are the location of human agency — the reason the MPCM boundary exists, and why the instrument panel always points beyond itself toward embodied, relational, somatic life.

The simulation is a finger pointing at the moon.

***

### Using these in a learning context

**As an opening:** Run the demo arc in Simulation 2 with no explanation. Ask learners what they noticed. Name the states afterward.

**As a diagnostic:** Ask learners to set the sliders to match how their _information environment_ feels right now — not how they feel personally, but the environment they spend most time in. What attractor basin does their configuration produce?

**As a closing:** Return to either simulation after a learning experience. Has anything shifted? Which slider moved most? The body knows before the framework names it.

**The one question that applies at every preset:**

> _Does this open movement, or close it?_
