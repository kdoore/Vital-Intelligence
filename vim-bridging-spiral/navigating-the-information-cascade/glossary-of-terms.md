# Glossary of Terms

### Bridging Spiral SR2 · Humanity++ · Cross-Domain Reference

_This glossary spans neuroscience, information theory, complex systems, machine learning, contemplative science, and arts-based research. Learning the vocabulary across these domains is itself a demonstration of the framework — the desirable difficulty of holding multiple representational spaces simultaneously is the precondition for the conceptual moves the framework makes._

***

### A

**Active Inference** A theory of how living systems maintain their organisation by minimising the difference between what they predict and what they experience (free energy). Under threat, systems minimise prediction error by controlling their environment (making the world match the prediction). Under safety, they minimise it by updating their models (learning). The balance between these two strategies is a core parameter of the framework. _Source: Friston, K. (2010). The free-energy principle: A unified brain theory. Nature Reviews Neuroscience._

**Aperture (A)** In the Bridging Spiral framework, the learning rate — how much new signal is integrated per encounter. Has three faces: somatic (Aₛ), cognitive (A꜀), and relational (Aᵣ). Trauma dysregulates aperture. Contemplative practice and psychological safety restore it. Corresponds to the learning rate hyperparameter in machine learning.

**Amoebic Motion** From the NBI framework: the characteristic movement pattern of a genuinely holarchic information system — organic, incorporating, slightly irregular, preserving the wholeness of the network while moving toward an objective. Distinguished from grid-lock (crystalline, symmetric, non-transformative circulation in a closed system). _Source: Natural Born Intelligence research._

**Attractor** In dynamical systems, a state or pattern toward which a system naturally tends to move. Systems can have multiple attractors — the dominance hierarchy and holarchic flow are both attractors in the DIKW Toroid simulation. Which attractor a system settles into depends on initial conditions and small perturbations at critical transition points.

***

### B

**Backpropagation (Backprop)** The primary learning algorithm in artificial neural networks. Requires: (1) a forward pass through the network, (2) a frozen snapshot of all activations, (3) a globally defined error signal, (4) a mathematically exact backward pass propagating that error through every layer simultaneously. Human learning does not work this way — it is continuous, local, and state-dependent. The distinction matters for AI literacy: human learning is not a slower, noisier version of backprop. It is structurally different.

**Bayesian Brain** The theoretical model in which the brain is fundamentally a prediction machine — constantly generating predictions about incoming sensory data and updating those predictions based on prediction error. Related to active inference and predictive processing. _Source: Clark, A. (2016). Surfing Uncertainty. Oxford University Press._

***

### C

**Causal Emergence** The mathematical demonstration that macro-level organisation can have greater causal power than the sum of its micro-level components. Challenges strict reductionism. Grounds the claim that holarchic systems are not merely decorative — they have independent causal efficacy that their components do not. _Source: Hoel, E.P., Albantakis, L., & Tononi, G. (2013). PNAS._

**Closed Representational Space** From the NBI framework: a system that optimises and generalises within fixed categories without transforming those categories. All operations relate known to known. The genuine "outside" — information that does not fit existing categories — is progressively eliminated through assimilation (correlationism). The Giant Pumpkin simulation mode. Distinguished from an open representational space which transforms its own categories through the Neither/Nor mechanism.

**Commitment Pooling** From Grassroots Economics research: a fundamental protocol for holarchic resource distribution, observed in both mycorrhizal fungal networks and ancient human traditions (e.g., Mweria in East Africa). Defined as an intangible common-pool of trusted promises that creates non-zero-sum connections without requiring centralised ownership or exchange medium. The social-economic model for generative resonance. _Source: Grassroots Economics research on Community Inclusion Currencies._

**Correlationism** The tendency of a closed system to relate everything to everything else — producing apparent openness and inclusivity while actually eliminating genuine difference. Everything becomes relatable; nothing is truly other. A signature of sophisticated extractive mimicry. The echo chamber at theoretical scale.

**The Crack** From the NBI framework: the productive rupture in a system's representational space through which genuine novelty enters. Requires active disequilibration — the Neither/Nor moment where the system briefly releases its existing categories and enters the vacant-place state. Cannot occur in a closed system. The transition event between Giant Pumpkin and Commitment Pool modes in the Pass 3 simulation. _See also: Vacant-Place State, Neither/Nor._

### D

**Desirable Difficulties** From cognitive science and educational psychology: challenges that slow apparent learning in the short term but produce more durable and transferable understanding in the long term. Includes interleaving, spacing, retrieval practice, and — in the framework's extension — the tolerated disequilibration at the K→U threshold. The term captures why genuine learning is uncomfortable: it requires the crack to open. _Source: Bjork, R.A. (1994). Memory and metamemory considerations in the training of human beings._

**Disequilibration** From the NBI framework: a state of productive cognitive and physiological destabilisation — characterised by high arousal co-occurring with active regulation — that precedes genuine transformative learning. Distinguished from distress (high arousal without regulation) and from the maze state (low arousal, smooth flow within fixed categories). Measured physiologically via Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD) of cardiac autonomic markers. _See also: Meditation Paradox, MAD._

**DIKW Stack** Data → Information → Knowledge → Understanding → Wisdom. A hierarchical model of how raw signal becomes meaningful through successive layers of processing. Data: raw signal, noise. Information: detected pattern. Knowledge: stable, validated model. Understanding: contextualised, embodied, requires somatic nonlinearity. Wisdom: integrated across time, relation, and lived experience. AI systems reliably reach Knowledge; Understanding and Wisdom require the living system. _Source: Ackoff, R.L. (1989). From data to wisdom. Journal of Applied Systems Analysis._

**Dominance Hierarchy** A system organised around the concentration of resources, information, and decision-making at a central node, maintained through threat and fear rather than voluntary commitment. In the DIKW Toroid simulation: the toroid collapses, upper DIKW levels are suppressed, particles flow inward. The PSI Theory equivalent: cortisol-driven regression to lower-level habits, with higher-order systems (Extension Memory, Self) inaccessible. _Contrast: Holarchy._

### E

**Edge of Chaos** _See: Self-Organised Criticality._

**Extension Memory (EM)** From PSI Theory: the extended associative network that enables holistic overview and remote association detection — the capacity to find unexpected connections across distant conceptual domains. Requires positive affect and psychological safety to activate. Suppressed by stress and negative affect (which produce tunnel vision instead). The neurological substrate of what the framework calls imagination. _Source: Kuhl, J., Quirin, M., & Koole, S.L. (2020). Advances in Motivation Science._

### F

**Free Energy Principle** _See: Active Inference._

**Free Energy (FEP context)** In the active inference framework: a measure of the difference between a system's internal model of the world and the actual state of the world (surprise). Living systems are organised to minimise free energy — either by updating their models or by acting to make the world match their predictions. High free energy = high prediction error = system under stress. The active inference readout in the DIKW Toroid Pass 2 simulation.

### G

**Giant Pumpkin** From Grassroots Economics research: a metaphor for pathological centralisation — a system in which all nutrients flow to a single node, producing extraordinary growth at the centre while exhausting the network's distributed abundance. In the Pass 3 simulation: the high-resonance, apparently-kind, internally-coherent extractive attractor. Beautiful. Self-confirming. Ultimately destructive of the network that feeds it. _Contrast: Commitment Pool._

### H

**Holarchy** A system of nested wholes — each level is simultaneously a complete whole and a part of a larger whole. Distinguished from both hierarchy (top-down control) and flat networks (no integration). Preserves the integrity of each level while enabling coordination across levels. Coined by Arthur Koestler. In the framework: the attractor toward which increasing kindness, genuine imagination, and generative resonance tend — when the values vector is oriented toward care rather than extraction.

**Holonomy** From differential geometry and the punctuated geometry framework: the angular mismatch or phase shift that occurs when information is transported around a defect point (puncture) in a system. Zero holonomy = the system has cycled back to its starting configuration — no genuine transformation occurred, the representational space is closed. Non-zero structured holonomy = genuine transformation — the system has exited through the crack into a new geometric regime. Used in Pass 3 as the mathematical structure underlying the crack mechanism, expressed visually rather than mathematically.

### I

**Imagination (✦)** In the Bridging Spiral framework: the latent space — the capacity to hold unrealised possibility. Not a dial but the negative space between dials. Has two modes: _generative imagination_ (creates new configurations within existing representational space — Both A and B) and _transformative imagination_ (breaks the representational space itself — Neither A nor B). Directionless — amplifies whatever values are present. Corresponds to the latent space in generative AI models. _Shadow: elaboration within a closed system._

**Inter-Brain Synchrony** From relational neuroscience: the neural entrainment that occurs between two people in genuine social contact — measurable as correlated brain activity patterns. Associated with empathy, shared understanding, and effective communication. Disrupted by stress and fear. Strengthened by safety and contemplative practice. The neurological substrate of resonance in the framework. _Source: De Felice, S. et al. (2025). Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews._

### K

**Kindness (❄)** In the Bridging Spiral framework: a field condition, not a personality trait. The environmental quality that makes membrane permeability possible without dissolution — that allows the system to be open without being overwhelmed. Operationalised as the combination of warm truth, firm limits, and repair. The regulatory condition for the somatic layer. _Shadow: xenophobic kindness — in-group warmth that progressively closes the outside._

**K→U Threshold** The boundary between Knowledge and Understanding in the DIKW stack. The most significant threshold in the framework: AI systems reliably reach K; genuine Understanding requires somatic nonlinearity (the activation function), tolerated disequilibration, and sufficient aperture. Marked in the Pass 2 simulation as the desirable difficulties zone — visible as a dashed ring in the self-organised criticality band.

### L

**Latent Space** In machine learning: the compressed internal representation space of a generative model — the space of encoded possibilities from which outputs are generated. In the framework: the correlate of imagination — the space of unrealised possibility that genuine creativity draws from. Accessing the latent space requires aperture, safety, and the willingness to enter the vacant-place state.

**Loss Function** In machine learning: the mathematical function that measures how far the model's output is from the desired output — the error signal that drives learning through backpropagation. In the framework: corresponds to the ♥ Relational Compass — the values that define what counts as error and what counts as success. The direction of the loss function determines whether a high-performing system is oriented toward care or extraction. The most important single parameter in the shadow dynamics analysis.

### M

**MAD (Mean Absolute Deviation)** A statistical measure of variability: the average distance of each data point from the mean of the dataset. In the Bellaiche cardiac autonomic research: high MAD of heart rate variability during creative engagement predicts greater anxiety reduction and creative output than low-MAD (stable, low-variability) engagement. In the framework: the volatility waveform in Pass 3 uses MAD as the signature of genuine disequilibration — distinguishing the alive, crack-prone, transformative state from the smooth, stable, maze state. _High MAD + regulation = genuine holarchy. Low MAD = maze._

**Markov Blanket** From statistical physics and active inference: the minimal set of variables that statistically separates a system from its environment — the boundary that defines what is "inside" and what is "outside." Everything inside the blanket is conditionally independent of everything outside, given the blanket states. In the framework: the boundary of self — permeable, governable, and shaped by kindness (permeability), imagination (what can be received), and aperture (integration bandwidth). _Source: Friston, K. (2013). Life as we know it. Journal of the Royal Society Interface._

**Meditation Paradox** From NBI research and the Bellaiche study: the finding that high physiological arousal co-occurring with active regulation — rather than low-arousal calm — is the signature state of genuine transformative learning and creative breakthrough. Inverts the common assumption that relaxation is the goal of contemplative practice. In the framework: the disequilibration dial's productive zone — where the system is genuinely destabilised and simultaneously held. _See also: Disequilibration, MAD._

**MPCM** Material → Process → Context → Meaning. The four-layer model of human meaning-making that anchors the entire framework. Engineers and AI systems operate in M and P. C and M require a living system. The MPCM boundary is the threshold where processing ends and meaning begins — or where it fails to begin. The central argument of the Human ↔ AI Coupling simulation.

**Mycelium Network** The underground fungal communication and nutrient-exchange network connecting trees and plants in a forest ecosystem. A biological model for holarchic distributed intelligence: scale-free, without central control, resilient through redundancy, creating non-zero-sum exchanges between nodes. In the framework: the visual language for the Commitment Pool attractor — the mycelium threads between nodes in the DIKW Toroid simulations represent genuine resonance connections as distinct from extraction spines.

### N

**NBI (Natural Born Intelligence)** A theoretical framework distinguishing human biological intelligence from artificial intelligence based on the capacity for non-monotonic transformation of representational space. AI-thinking operates within fixed representational spaces (optimisation and generalisation). NBI-thinking transforms the space itself through the Neither/Nor mechanism, generating an "outside" domain not contained in the original categories. The source of the crack concept and the A/B two-layer engine. _Source: Natural Born Intelligence research papers in the studio research bibliography._

**Neither/Nor** _See: The Crack, Vacant-Place State._ The logical and experiential state in which a system temporarily releases both A and B — neither confirming nor denying — to allow for genuine transformation of the representational space. Distinguished from Both/And (co-applicability within existing categories). The productive void that precedes genuine novelty.

**Negentropy** The opposite of entropy — the tendency of living systems to maintain and increase their internal organisation against the general thermodynamic tendency toward disorder. In the framework: the left axis of the entropy/negentropy waveform. Wisdom and genuine holarchy tend toward negentropy — they sustain coherent organisation without central control. The commitment pool circulates energy; the giant pumpkin increases local order at the cost of network-wide entropy.

### P

**Predictive Coding** A theory of neural processing in which each layer of the brain generates predictions about the layer below, and learning occurs through the propagation of local prediction errors. A more biologically plausible model than backpropagation. Key distinction: learning occurs continuously and locally, not in phase-switched global passes. Supports the framework's claim that human learning requires distributed local agency, not central control. _Source: Clark, A. (2016). Surfing Uncertainty._

**PSI Theory (Personality Systems Interactions Theory)** A motivational architecture theory proposing seven levels of human agency, from basic sensorimotor habits (Level 1) to Self-Management (Level 7). High stress/cortisol causes regression — higher-level systems lose their ability to modulate lower-level habits. Safety enables progression — higher-level systems, including Extension Memory and the Self, become accessible. The empirical grounding for the DIKW suppression under dominance in the simulation. _Source: Kuhl, J., Quirin, M., & Koole, S.L. (2020). Advances in Motivation Science._

**Puncture / Punctuated Geometry** From mathematical physics: a measure-zero defect in a manifold — a structured transition point where the geometric regime changes from smooth/weak curvature to strong/concentrated curvature. Not an absence but a structured transition layer. In the framework: the mathematical description of the crack — a point where the information space's geometry transforms, with non-zero holonomy indicating genuine rather than apparent transformation. Used as design metaphor rather than biological proof in the current simulations.

### R

**Resonance (◎)** In the Bridging Spiral framework: the degree to which nodes in a network adopt shared oscillation patterns. Has two distinct modes: _entrainment resonance_ (synchronisation within fixed categories — echo chamber, maze, cult formation) and _generative resonance_ (phase-locking between systems with genuinely different internal spaces — produces novelty at the interface). The distinction between these two modes is the shadow parameter problem for resonance. High resonance without a care-oriented values vector = mob dynamics. _See also: Inter-Brain Synchrony, Commitment Pooling._

**Regression (PSI Theory)** The state in which stress or cortisol attenuates the influence of higher-level motivational systems, causing behaviour to be governed by lower-level habits and emotional impulses regardless of stated intentions. The PSI Theory grounding for the DIKW suppression under dominance conditions. _Contrast: Progression._

***

### S

**Self-Infiltration** From PSI Theory: the state in which a person under threat cannot distinguish their own goals from externally imposed goals — because they lack access to the Self system (Extension Memory) required for self-other differentiation. The neurological mechanism by which dominance systems capture individual cognition without explicit force. Explains how cult members, nationalist movement participants, and recommendation-algorithm-radicalised users _feel_ like they are acting from their own values while executing someone else's. The mechanism behind the xenophobic kindness shadow parameter.

**Self-Organised Criticality (SOC)** The property of certain dynamical systems to naturally evolve toward a critical state — the edge of chaos — at which point small perturbations can produce effects of any magnitude (including avalanches). At SOC, the system is maximally sensitive to perturbation, maximally flexible, and capable of the most complex responses. In the framework: the SOC zone is the band of self-organised criticality visible in the entropy/negentropy waveform — the yellow band where the crack is possible, desirable difficulties are active, and an avalanche of kindness becomes physically plausible. _Source: Bak, P. (1996). How Nature Works. Sbitnev (2024)._

**Somatic Gyroscope (♠)** The first instrument of the Bridging Spiral dashboard. Corresponds to the nonlinear activation function in deep neural networks — the component without which multiple layers collapse to a single linear transformation regardless of depth. Operationalises somatic awareness, interoception, and embodied regulation. The layer that stress and trauma suppress first, and that contemplative and somatic practice most directly restores. Gated by the ♠ sub-dials: Proprioceptive Intelligence (PI), Window of Tolerance (WT), and Felt Sense (FE).

### T

**Technosocial Phase Transformation** A proposed large-scale transition in the organisation of human information systems — from dominance-hierarchy attractors (centralised, extractive, suppressing upper DIKW levels in populations) toward holarchic attractors (distributed, generative, making Understanding and Wisdom accessible). Understood thermodynamically: requires sufficient energy input (disequilibration, investment of attention and care) to overcome the activation barrier between attractors. The framework's largest-scale claim — grounded in complex systems theory, prosocial economics, and contemplative neuroscience, but currently theoretical rather than empirically demonstrated.

**Tunnel Vision** From PSI Theory: the cognitive state produced by high stress and negative affect, in which the brain's processing narrows to isolated objects and error detection, losing access to the holistic pattern recognition of Extension Memory. The shadow of imagination under dominance conditions. Useful for threat response; destructive for creative recombination and systems thinking. The cognitive mode that dominance systems depend on maintaining in their populations.

### U

**Understanding (U)** The fourth level of the DIKW stack. Distinguished from Knowledge by its requirement for somatic engagement — embodied contextualisation, connection to lived experience, integration across multiple representational frameworks. Not computable by current AI systems. Requires the Neither/Nor mechanism — the willingness to have existing categories temporarily suspended. The level at which the K→U threshold marks the desirable difficulty zone.

***

### V

**Vacant-Place State** From NBI framework: the productive emptiness that occurs when the Neither/Nor mechanism activates — the brief suspension in which existing categories are released before a new, transformed configuration assembles. Not absence of content but structured openness to genuine novelty. Visualised in the Pass 3 simulation as the luminous grey-white bloom during the crack transition — the two seconds between Giant Pumpkin and Commitment Pool in which the system is neither. _See also: The Crack, Neither/Nor._

**Values Vector** The direction of a system's loss function — what the system's learning process is oriented toward maximising or minimising. The critical missing parameter in early versions of the DIKW Toroid simulation. High kindness + imagination + resonance + care-oriented values vector → holarchic flow. High kindness + imagination + resonance + extraction-oriented values vector → sophisticated dominance mimicking holarchy. The most important single distinguishing feature between genuine holarchy and its extractive imitations.

**VUCA** Volatile, Uncertain, Complex, Ambiguous. A framework from military strategic planning (US Army War College, 1987) describing the post-Cold War operational environment, now widely applied to contemporary information ecosystems, organisational contexts, and educational challenges. The environmental condition that makes AI literacy critical and that the Bridging Spiral framework is specifically designed to navigate.

***

### W

**Window of Tolerance** From trauma therapy (Siegel, Ogden): the zone of arousal in which a person can function effectively — not so under-aroused as to be dissociated, not so over-aroused as to be reactive or flooded. Within the window: the capacity for reflection, empathy, and learning is available. Outside the window: the system defaults to lower-level responses. Corresponds to the SOC zone in the framework — the productive disequilibration zone is within the window of tolerance, not beyond it. _Source: Siegel, D.J. (1999). The Developing Mind._

**Wisdom (W)** The fifth and highest level of the DIKW stack. Requires: temporal depth (integration over time), relational accountability (calibrated by the ♥ Relational Compass), embodied understanding (through the ♠ Somatic Gyroscope), and the capacity for repair (♣ Dimensional Integration). Not computable. Not transferable through instruction. Developed through sustained practice across the full instrument stack under conditions of psychological safety and genuine disequilibration. The attractor toward which the kindness field condition, when functioning holarchically, tends.

***

### X

**Xenophobic Kindness** The shadow parameter of kindness: in-group warmth that co-evolves with out-group dehumanisation. From evolutionary biology (Tomasello) and social psychology (Tajfel & Turner): the capacity for "survival of the friendliest" within a group tends to develop alongside increased hostility toward those defined as outside the group. High kindness parameter + purity-oriented values vector = warmth that closes the outside rather than opens it. Explains how communities with high internal resonance and apparent kindness can produce violence toward outsiders. The mechanism behind cult warmth, nationalist community feeling, and tribally-coherent disinformation ecosystems.
